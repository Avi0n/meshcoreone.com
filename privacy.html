Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 8, 2026

MeshCore One ("the app", "MC1") is an offline-first MeshCore client for iPhone, iPad, and Mac. It is built to operate without the internet, and it is designed around a simple principle: your data stays on your device. The app has no user accounts, no backend servers operated by us, and no analytics or third-party tracking.

01Summary

MeshCore One does not collect, transmit, or sell your personal information to us or to any third party. The app communicates with your MeshCore radio hardware and with the mesh network itself, not with servers we control. Everything the app stores lives in your device's local app storage and is removed when you delete the app.

02Data stored on your device

To function, the app stores the following locally on your device only:

  • Messages and conversations: direct messages, channels, and room-server history you send or receive.
  • Contacts and nodes: names, public keys, and metadata for contacts and repeaters discovered on the mesh.
  • Network & diagnostic data: saved paths, RX logs, telemetry history, and line-of-sight results you generate.
  • App settings: your preferences, radio presets, and connection configuration.

This data is never uploaded to us. You can export or delete it from within the app, and uninstalling the app erases it from your device.

03Data sent over the mesh

MeshCore One is a messaging client, so content you choose to send (messages, reactions, your shared position, adverts, and node commands) is transmitted over the MeshCore radio network to its intended recipients. MeshCore uses end-to-end encryption for direct and private messages; public channels are, by design, readable by anyone with the channel key.

Because the mesh is a decentralized radio network, messages may be relayed by intermediate repeater nodes operated by other people in order to reach their destination. We do not operate this network and cannot see, log, or control its traffic.

04Location

Location access is optional and used only for features you explicitly enable, such as sharing your position with contacts or placing your node on the map. Your location is used on-device and is only transmitted over the mesh when you choose to share it. The app does not send your location to us or to any analytics service. You can revoke location access at any time in iOS Settings.

05Push notifications

If you enable notifications, iOS issues a push token through Apple Push Notification service (APNs) so the app can alert you to new messages. This token is handled by Apple's infrastructure and is not used by us to identify or track you. Message content for local alerts is processed on your device. You can disable notifications at any time in iOS Settings.

06Bluetooth & local network

The app uses Bluetooth and your local network (WiFi) solely to discover and communicate with your MeshCore companion hardware. These permissions are required to pair and exchange data with your radio. No Bluetooth or network data is collected by us or shared with third parties.

07What we do not collect

  • No accounts, sign-ups, email addresses, or phone numbers.
  • No analytics, telemetry, advertising identifiers, or behavioral tracking.
  • No cloud backup of your messages or contacts on servers we operate.
  • No sale or sharing of personal data, because there is none to sell.

08Third-party services

The app relies on a small number of platform services to function:

  • Apple: for app distribution (TestFlight / App Store), push notification delivery (APNs), and map tiles when you use the map. Apple's handling of this data is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.
  • Map imagery: standard, satellite, and hybrid map tiles are provided by Apple Maps and loaded only when you open the map.

We do not embed third-party advertising or analytics SDKs.

09Security

Message encryption is provided by the MeshCore protocol for direct and private communications. Data stored on your device is protected by iOS's standard app sandboxing and device-level encryption. Because the app is open source, anyone can review exactly how it handles data. No system is perfectly secure, and public channels are intentionally readable by anyone holding the channel key.

10Children

MeshCore One is a general-purpose communications and radio tool and is not directed at children. It does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13.

11Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected here with an updated date above. Because the project is open source, the full revision history is available in the project's public repository.

12Contact

MeshCore One is a free and open-source project. Questions about privacy can be raised as an issue on the project's GitHub repository.
